## 1. Desire Stack → Obstacle → Solution

**When to use:** Top-of-funnel ads, landing page heroes, intro emails, cold outreach openers.

**Psychology:** Stack three escalating desires so the reader nods "yes, yes, YES" — then name the one obstacle standing in their way. By the time you introduce the solution, buying feels like the obvious next step.

**Template:**

> Do you want [specific desire #1]?
> Do you want [specific desire #2]?
> Do you want [bigger-picture desire #3]?
>
> Chances are you want [ultimate goal], but [obstacle they relate to].
>
> Introducing [solution] — [one-line value prop].

## 2. Pain → Agitate → Solution (PAS)

**When to use:** Email subject lines, retargeting ads, sales pages, social captions targeting aware audiences.

**Psychology:** Name a pain they already feel, twist the knife just enough to create urgency, then present the relief.

**Template:**

> [Pain statement — something they experience daily.]
>
> [Agitate — what happens if they keep ignoring it. Make the cost real.]
>
> [Solution — your offer framed as the fastest path out.]

## 3. Before → After → Bridge (BAB)

**When to use:** Case studies, testimonials, email sequences, landing page sections.

**Psychology:** Paint the "before" so they feel seen, paint the "after" so they feel desire, then bridge the gap with your offer.

**Template:**

> **Before:** [Their current frustrating reality.]
> **After:** [The specific, tangible outcome they want.]
> **Bridge:** [How your offer gets them there.]

**AW Media example:**

> **Before:** You're spending 10+ hours a week on marketing — DIY Canva graphics, captions written at midnight, a website you haven't touched since 2023 — and you're still not getting the leads you need.

> **After:** Your brand looks sharp. Your content posts itself. Leads come in while you sleep. You actually feel like the professional you are.

> **Bridge:** AW Media takes over your entire marketing operation — strategy, design, content, and ads — so the "after" becomes your Monday morning reality.

## 4. The "One Belief" Framework

**When to use:** High-ticket offers, webinar pitches, long-form sales pages, VSLs.

**Psychology:** If you can change one core belief, the sale follows. Identify the false belief holding them back, break it, and replace it with a belief that makes your offer the logical next step.

**Template:**

> Most [audience] believe [common false belief].
>
> Here's the truth: [reality reframe + proof].
>
> That's why [solution] works — it's built around [the new belief], not [the old one].

**AW Media example:**

> Most small business owners believe they need to "go viral" to grow.
>
> Here's the truth: the businesses making $10K–$50K/month from social media have never gone viral. They have clear offers, consistent content that positions them as the authority, and a system that turns followers into clients — not fans.
>
> That's why AW Media's approach works — we build marketing systems around conversion, not clout.

## 5. The "What If" Opener

**When to use:** Ad hooks, email openers, carousel slide one, Reels opening line.

**Psychology:** "What if" bypasses skepticism because it asks the reader to imagine rather than believe. It opens a mental door without triggering resistance.

**Template:**

> What if [desirable outcome] — without [the thing they hate doing]?

**AW Media examples:**

> What if your Instagram actually brought in clients — without you posting every single day?

> What if your brand looked like a million-dollar company — without the million-dollar budget?

> What if you could stop worrying about marketing entirely — and it still worked?

## 6. The "You Don't Need X — You Need Y" Reframe

**When to use:** Objection handling, contrarian social posts, ad copy that differentiates.

**Psychology:** Repositions the problem so your offer becomes the obvious answer. Works especially well when competitors are all selling "X."

**Template:**

> You don't need [what they think they need].
> You need [what they actually need].
>
> [One sentence explaining why, then CTA.]

**AW Media examples:**

> You don't need another logo redesign. You need an offer so clear that a stranger can explain it in one sentence.

> You don't need more followers. You need the 200 you already have to actually know what you sell — and want it.

> You don't need a content calendar. You need a content system that adapts, converts, and doesn't fall apart when life gets busy.

## Quick Reference: Which Framework for Which Situation

| Situation | Best Framework | Why |
|-----------|---------------|-----|
| Cold audience ad | Desire Stack | Builds agreement before asking anything |
| Retargeting ad | PAS | They already know the pain — amplify it |
| Case study / testimonial | BAB | Shows transformation with proof |
| Sales page or webinar | One Belief | Shifts the worldview that blocks the sale |
| Hook for any content | What If | Low-resistance curiosity trigger |
| Objection handling | You Don't Need X | Reframes what they think the problem is |
| Email welcome sequence | Desire Stack → PAS → BAB | Escalate across 3 emails |
| Landing page hero | Desire Stack or What If | Grabs attention above the fold |
| Social caption | PAS or Reframe | Punchy, fits short-form |

## Stacking Frameworks Across a Funnel

A single framework wins a single moment. Stack them across the customer journey to win the sale:

**Ad (Desire Stack)** → "Do you want... Do you want... Do you want..."
↓
**Landing Page Hero (What If)** → "What if you could..."
↓
**Landing Page Body (BAB)** → Before / After / Bridge with case study
↓
**Objection Section (Reframe)** → "You don't need X — you need Y"
↓
**Close (One Belief)** → Break the one belief standing between them and the buy button
↓
**Follow-up Email (PAS)** → Pain, agitate, final CTA
